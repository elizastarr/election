Meet the candidates

Tripp Keeffe

Candidate for President

Tripp Keeffe is a rising junior from Elgin, South Carolina. He is majoring in Political Science with a concentration in politics, philosophy, and law. Outside of Student Government, Tripp enjoys traveling, running, and going to the beach with his friends and family! Tripp currently serves as the Student Government Vice President. In this role, he has established a CARTA route to Harris Teeter free for students, negotiated an agreement to provide free rides from Charleston Green Taxi for students, and co-led a committee that brought guest meal swipes for all meal plans to campus. Additionally, he has represented students to various administrators regarding initiatives to enhance The College’s diversity. Every Tuesday, Tripp has led the student Senate overseeing the passage of all legislation. In prior years, he served as Committee Director for Student Government in which he acquired more furniture for Stern Center Gardens, expanded rental of technology in the library, and helped keep our campus cleaner by adding more trash cans. Tripp’s experience and commitment to serving the College of Charleston makes him the most qualified candidate to be your student body President.

Tripp Keeffe

Candidate for Vice-President

Tripp Keeffe currently serves as an executive initiative director for the Student Government Association. In this role, he leads a committee to institute positive changes on campus. Some of his achievements in this role include bringing new outdoor seating to the Stern Center Gardens, expanding technology rentals available in the library, and bringing free reusable bags to campus. He also is currently working to improve the accuracy of CARTA shuttles and open a CARTA route to a grocery store. Before he was a committee director, he worked as a senator to bring more trashcans to campus to cut down on littering and, for his efforts and work with the student government, was named SGA senator of the month. Outside of SGA, Tripp volunteers at the Allegro Charter School of Music, mentoring at-risk children from the Charleston community. He also serves as the programming director for the College Debate Society, and in this role, has planned several successful events for this organization. He is majoring in political science with a minor in Spanish, and he is passionate about civil service. When not in class or extracurricular programs, Tripp enjoys playing guitar, listening to music, running, and just hanging out with friends. Due to his wealth of experience and passion for giving back to the Charleston community, Tripp Keeffe is the best choice for Student Body Vice President!

Platform

Making our Campus a Community

People talk a lot about the campus community. But what does that mean? To us it means a student government that brings your concerns to the forefront, where your representatives advocate for the issues that affect all of us. And that's what we want to do with this campaign. We want to make our campus a community. And here are some of the steps we will take to do this if elected.

Transportation

We want to make it easier for students to navigate this campus so that they can branch out, travel throughout the community, and get the most out of their college experience. Below are some of our plans to accomplish this:


  • We are currently working to implement a CARTA route to a grocery store, so that students who may currently be unable to go purchase groceries, perhaps due to a disability or lack of a car or bike, will not be prevented from accessing these necessities.

  • We want to expand the Cougar Shuttle into more than a one-person operation, and partner with Uber or Lyft to provide students discounts for travelling around the Charleston area. These improvements will increase the choices and affordability of transportation for students.

  • Many students get around campus using skateboards and longboards, but have nowhere to store them when they go to class or on-campus events. To remedy this, we want to install skateboard racks around campus.

Academics

We want to make sure that no students are held back from achieving their full academic potential here at the College of Charleston. To this end, these are our plans to increase academic excellence for everyone here at the College:


  • Many opportunities for professional development or post-graduate engagement, including jobs, internships, and study abroad programs, require official academic transcripts. However, academic transcripts cannot be ordered without paying a fee, and we believe that no student should be hindered from academic and professional excellence by their financial situation. Therefore, if elected, we will ensure students are provided at least two free academic transcripts throughout their college career.

  • We want students to have maximum control over their schedules and flexibility to choose the classes that are best for them. However, currently, the add-drop period fluctuates between 4 and 6 days depending on the semester, giving students not only a small amount of time to decide if they want to change their schedule, but also an uncertain window of opportunity. To fix this, we will standardize and extend the add-drop period to 7 days, every semester, to ensure students have maximum control over their own schedules here at the college.

Student Involvement

We have great organizations and communities here on campus, and we believe it is important for student government to be responsive to these groups and organizations in order to foster greater student involvement in this community. Here are some of our ideas on how to do that:


  • Clubs and organizations do great things on this campus, but can at times feel as though these achievements go unrecognized. To remedy this and to incentivize organizations to go out and do great things in our community, we will institute a Club of the Week program which will recognize and reward clubs and organizations that do great things in our community. These things can include breaking philanthropy records, selling out tickets to an event, or just involving a large number of students in an event on campus. Got any ideas about who should be the first club of the week? Let us know!

  • We have a lot of diverse communities here at the College of Charleston that deserve a greater voice in campus affairs. That is why we will reorganize the Student Government’s cabinet and liaison system to hear and address concerns from diverse, and often underrepresented, groups on campus.


Safety

All students are entitled to feel safe on this campus, and it is a priority of this campaign to ensure that safety is promoted and fostered on this campus. Here is how we plan to do that:


  • We want to institute an app, available to all students, that will serve as  a communications platform to promote safety and prevention efforts.

  • Additionally, St. Philip Street and Coming Street are two of the most heavily trafficked roads on campus. Many students need to cross these streets to make it to class on time, but currently cars are very reluctant to stop and allow students to cross, which can become a safety issue when students must cross anyway to get to class. Therefore, we want to put stop signs at the crosswalks on St. Philip Street and Coming Street.

Fun

We believe the campus should be a fun and relaxing place to spend your time.


  • To this end, we would like to put eno posts in the Stern Gardens and establish an Eno Rental System in the Stern Student Center so that students can have a fun way to relax and study.

You Tell Us!

A student government that responds to, and advocates for, its constituents is something we believe in and want to embody, so we want to hear from you! Feel free to send us any concerns you have about campus that you’d like us to address in our platform and with our candidacy.
